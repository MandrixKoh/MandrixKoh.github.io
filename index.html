Gardening 101

Types of Gardens

Flower Garden

A flower garden is a vibrant oasis of color, fragrance, and life, where blooms like roses, tulips, and daisies create a stunning display.
Whether in backyard beds, containers, or wildflower meadows, flowers attract pollinators, uplift moods,
and transform outdoor spaces into natural works of art. With the right care—sunlight, water, and soil—anyone can cultivate a thriving floral paradise.

Vegetable Garden

Vegetable gardening turns your backyard, balcony, or even a sunny windowsill into a source of fresh, homegrown food.
From crisp lettuce and juicy tomatoes to spicy peppers and sweet carrots, growing your own veggies saves money,
boosts health, and connects you to nature. With good soil, sunlight, and a little patience,
anyone can harvest the rewards of their own edible garden—one delicious bite at a time.

Container Garden

Container gardening brings fresh herbs, flowers, and veggies to life—no yard needed! Perfect for patios, balconies, or windowsills,
pots and planters let you grow anything from basil and cherry tomatoes to vibrant petunias with just soil, sunlight, and water.
Compact, versatile, and beginner-friendly, it's gardening made simple for any space.
